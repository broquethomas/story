Expectations

It's important to understand this training takes time. In addition, it will require edurance, patience and practice from both rider and horse to solidify the training.

Consistency is just as important as starting with a quality horse with a trainable personality.

We develop horses for open shows, futurity's, derby's, non-pros and youth. For use in cutting, reining, mounted shooting, cow horse and trail riding.

Training Methods

I have important beliefs that are the pillar of my training program. The program still needs to be adapted for every horse to bring out its best. This is what I believe differentiates me from other trainers.

1

Horses are ridden every day with turn outs on weekends to graze, run, play and relax.

2

Create a solid foundation with all horses to ensure safety and a quiet temperment

3

Consistency leads to success. Consistent training on a consistent schedule.

6

Mastering any specific manuever is not as imporant as overall growth in all aspects. Consistensy is important but variation is too.

5

Daily training with the intent of the horse going to a show pen. Maintains high motivation and consistent growth.

4

Pressure and Release: Let the wrong way feel difficult and the correct way feel easy.

7

The horse needs to know who to respect and to easily differentiate between right and wrong.

8

All horses will learn to be quiet, behaved and confident when on public trails.

9

Younger horses will be introduced to new training daily to ensure interest without bordom as well as challenging them so they do not develop bad habits.

10

Patience and Kindness. We must remember we are the stewards of these wonderful animals, treat them with respect and give them the absolute best care possible.
